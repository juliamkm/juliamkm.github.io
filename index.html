Backpacking the Bay Area

Where to Go & What to Bring

Within a One-Hour Drive

There are so many great places to go, it can be hard to decide where to start. The first question to ask yourself is how far you want to drive.
Within one hour, you could explore the Marin Headlands, the East Bay hills, or the South Bay.

Within a Two-Hour Drive

With a little bit more time, you could drive to Henry W. Coe State Park, Pfeiffer Big Sur State Park, the Santa Cruz Mountains, or Point Reyes National Seashore.

With your destination choosen, you can then search online or using a mapping app such as AllTrails to find a trail suitable to your skillset and timeframe.

Ten Essentials to Bring

When planning a backpacking trip, it can be handy to remember the "Ten Essentials." This includes navigation, headlamp, sun protection, first aid, knife, fire (includes matches, lighter, tinder and/or stove) shelter, extra food, and extra water.

Further Considerations

You will also want to take into consideration the weather forecast when planning for your trip. Cooler temperatures call for warmer layers, extra calories, and more fuel to cook and heat water.
Warmer temps mean staying covered from the sun, packing extra water, and being cautious of heat-related illness

Recreate outdoors responsibly. Always tell someone where you are going and by when you plan to be back.
For further reading, check out websites including The Outbound, Roots Rated, and the NorCal Hiking SubReddit.
